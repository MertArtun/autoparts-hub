How Brake Systems Work

Your car's brake system is a hydraulic system that uses fluid pressure to multiply the force from your foot on the pedal. Understanding this system helps you maintain it properly.

Key Components

Brake Pads: These press against the rotors to create friction and slow your vehicle. They wear down over time and need regular replacement.

Rotors: Metal discs that the brake pads clamp down on. They can become warped or scored and may need resurfacing or replacement.

Calipers: House the brake pads and use hydraulic pressure to force them against the rotors.

Brake Fluid: Transfers pressure from the pedal to the calipers. It should be flushed every 2-3 years.

Warning Signs

  • Squealing or grinding noises
  • Vibration when braking
  • Soft or spongy brake pedal
  • Vehicle pulling to one side
  • Warning lights on dashboard

Maintenance Schedule

Inspect brakes every 12,000 miles, replace pads every 25,000-70,000 miles depending on driving style, and flush brake fluid every 2-3 years.
