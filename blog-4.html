Battery Basics

Your car battery provides the electrical current needed to start the engine and power accessories. Most batteries last 3-5 years, but various factors can shorten this lifespan.

Warning Signs of a Failing Battery

  • Slow engine crank when starting
  • Dim headlights or interior lights
  • Battery warning light on dashboard
  • Swollen battery case
  • Corrosion on terminals
  • Age over 3 years

Testing Your Battery

Most auto parts stores offer free battery testing. You can also use a multimeter to check voltage. A healthy battery should read 12.6 volts or higher when the engine is off.

Extending Battery Life

Keep terminals clean and tight, avoid short trips that don't allow full recharging, turn off all accessories before shutting off the engine, and park in a garage during extreme weather when possible.

Choosing a Replacement

When buying a new battery, consider Cold Cranking Amps (CCA), Reserve Capacity (RC), and the warranty period. Always match or exceed your manufacturer's specifications.
